New Orleans Style Jambalaya

Jambalaya, the aromatic trinity of Cajun/Creole cooking: onion, celery, and bell peppers (capsicums), sauteed in andouille drippings with garlic, herbs and Cajun spices. An incredibly favorful dish.

Ingredients

    • Steps

    1. Preheat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add choice of protein and cook, tossing occasionally, about 5 - 6 minutes. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels to drain, leave oil in pan.
    2. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery to pot. Saute for 6 minutes. Add garlic and saute 1 minute longer.
    3. Pour in chicken broth, crushed tomatoes, paprika, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir in cooked sausage and rice.
    4. Bring mixture to a light boil over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to low heat, cover and simmer until rice is almost fully tender, about 20 - 25 minutes. Stir in a little extra broth as needed.
    5. Stir in shrimp and okra, cover and continue to cook until shrimp have cooked through, about 4 - 5 minutes.
    6. Stir in protein, green onions and parsley. Serve immediately garnishing each serving with more parsley if desired.
    7. Serve and enjoy!

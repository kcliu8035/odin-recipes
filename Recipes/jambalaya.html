How to make the Wonderful Jambalaya

Description

Jambalaya is delicious treat for you and the whole family!

Done correctly, it will send your taste buds on a mouth-watering, spicy trip!

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Choice of protein (sausage or chicken)
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 2 medium bell peppers (recommended: 1 red, 1 yellow)
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 3 celery ribs, chopped (1 cup)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced (1 1/2 Tbsp)
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth, plus more as needed
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can crushed tomatoes*
  • 1 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper, less or more to taste**
  • 1 1/2 cups long grain white rice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 lb. large or extra large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 1/2 cups sliced okra, fresh or frozen, thawed (optional)
  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (I use rotisserie chicken)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, plus more for garnish if desired
  • 1/3 cup chopped green onions

Steps

  1. Preheat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add sausage and cook, tossing occasionally until browned, about 5 - 6 minutes. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels to drain, leave oil in pan.
  2. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery to pot. Saute 6 minutes. Add garlic and saute 1 minute longer.
  3. Pour in chicken broth, crushed tomatoes, paprika, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir in cooked sausage and rice.
  4. Bring mixture to a light boil over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to low heat, cover and simmer until rice is almost fully tender, about 20 - 25 minutes. Stir in a little extra broth as needed.
  5. Stir in shrimp and okra, cover and continue to cook until shrimp have cooked through, about 4 - 5 minutes.
  6. Stir in chicken, green onions and parsley. Serve immediately garnishing each serving with more parsley if desired.

And there you have it, jambalaya!
